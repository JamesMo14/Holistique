Acupuncturist

Yvonne Liang

Trained in both Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) and Five Elements Acupuncture (Worsley method), Yvonne began her training in 2016 in Bangkok, Thailand, before graduating from the Asante Academy of Chinese Medicine in London. She is a registered member of the Acupuncture Society UK.

Her practice supports conditions including stress, anxiety, insomnia, hormonal imbalances, digestive issues, tension, and chronic pain—always with a holistic, patient-centred approach.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is acupuncture?
A traditional Chinese medicine practice that involves inserting fine, single-use sterile needles into specific points on the body to restore balance, relieve pain, and support the body’s natural healing processes. It’s commonly used for stress, anxiety, insomnia, hormonal imbalances, digestive issues, and musculoskeletal pain.
What conditions does acupuncture help with?
Acupuncture is widely used for pain relief—including back, neck, arthritis, headaches, fibromyalgia—and has WHO-recognised effectiveness for specific conditions such as chemotherapy-related nausea, depression, allergic rhinitis, induction of labour, gastrointestinal symptoms, and more.
Does acupuncture hurt?
Most people find acupuncture to be very gentle and relaxing. The needles are hair-thin, and sensations can vary from a dull ache to a slight tingling—often followed by a deep sense of calm.
What is fire cupping?
A technique that uses glass cups and heat to create suction on the skin. This promotes blood flow, releases tension in muscles, and helps move stagnation. It can leave temporary marks that fade in a few days.
What conditions is fire cupping good for?
Cupping is especially effective for muscle tightness, back and shoulder pain, tension headaches, and respiratory issues like colds or congestion. It’s often used alongside acupuncture for enhanced results.
What is the infrared heat lamp used for?
The infrared heat lamp delivers soothing, penetrating warmth that helps increase circulation, relax muscles, and support the flow of Qi (energy) in the body.
Are these treatments safe?
Yes, acupuncture, fire cupping, and infrared heat therapy are safe when performed by a trained practitioner. All needles are single-use and sterile. Cupping and infrared heat are applied mindfully based on your comfort and health needs.
How many sessions will I need?
This depends on your condition and wellness goals. Some people feel immediate relief, while others may benefit from a series of treatments to see lasting improvement.
What should I do after a treatment?
After your session, drink plenty of water and allow your body to rest. Avoid strenuous exercise, alcohol, and caffeine for a few hours.
How should I prepare for a session?
Avoid heavy meals, alcohol, or caffeine for at least 1–2 hours before your appointment. Try to come in a relaxed state, and wear loose, comfortable clothing.
